About Us

In the Spring of 2017, Principal Founder Kevin Nguyen had a dream to bring about a new opportunity for Asian American men on campus. He desired to leave behind a positive legacy through a unique brotherhood that defied the status quo. He also recognized that the University of Wisconsin-Madison lacked opportunities for Asian American men to find a healthy Greek life experience.

Determined to create that space, Kevin recruited a group of men who shared his vision: Dustin Nguyen, Timothy Tran, Daniel King, David Ho, Justyn Nguyen, Ly Hoang, Thang Nguyen, and Nawang Singhe. Together, they founded SOULL — Strength, Opportunity, Unity, Leadership, and Love — using these values to guide their mission and future members.

By Fall 2017, SOULL had grown with the additions of Brian Leung, Travis Dao, Hong Nhan, Nawal Dua, and Neehar Namjoshi. Their goal was to provide a platform for Asian American men to have a voice on campus and a safe, inclusive space to foster a new kind of brotherhood. Their efforts paid off when they were officially accepted into the Multicultural Greek Council on November 14, 2017.

In Spring 2018, they pursued the dream of joining a larger national organization. After months of dedication and hard work, they were officially recognized as the Chosen Charters of the 67th Chapter of Lambda Phi Epsilon International Fraternity, Inc. on April 15, 2018.
